sections:
  - heading: "Overview"
    body: "Sweetpea Wildwood is a bespoke woodworking studio rooted in care, craftsmanship, and endurance. Each piece is shaped by hand using honest materials, guided by patience and intention rather than speed or trends. From custom furniture to everyday objects, every piece is built to last, designed to hold meaning and made to live with you for years to come. The brand runs on five words: imperfect, unique, purposeful, utility, pride."
  - heading: "The mark"
    body: "A modern maker's mark: a hummingbird meets a blooming daffodil, encircled by a saw blade. The hummingbird is presence and persistence, the daffodil is renewal and the quiet return of light, and the blade honors the workshop and the precision of bespoke woodworking. Built to be stamped, burned, or engraved as a lasting signature rather than decoration."

  - heading: "Logo system"
    body: "Primary, secondary, and monochrome marks. The secondary mark is a rotating saw-blade ring whose center image can change (seasonal, project-specific, or narrative-driven) while the frame keeps it instantly recognizable. A flexible system that stays grounded across physical and digital applications."
    images:
      - { src: "images/logo-mark.png", alt: "Logo mark with monochrome variants" }

  - heading: "Color"
    body: "The palette is split into two worlds. One is vibrant and alive: teals, greens, and soft yellows drawn from the hummingbird and florals, reserved for storytelling and the brand's symbolic heart. The other is grounded in wood tones and muted neutrals (deep greens, warm browns, stone, and charcoal) that celebrate texture, grain, and honest craft. Together they balance what grows with what is built to last."

    body: "A type system built on contrast and restraint. Ohno Blazeface brings sculptural personality to display moments, Grad lends a calm and traditional voice to body copy, and Sweet Sans Pro keeps fine print clear and unobtrusive, a hierarchy that feels crafted rather than manufactured."
